What is a Sensor?

Defining Inputs

A sensor is a device that detects changes in the environment and sends the information to other electronics, frequently a computer processor.

Camera = Eyes

Visual Perception

Just like human eyes, cameras capture light to create images of the surrounding world.

Ultrasonic Sensor = Ears

How robots detect distance using sound

Ultrasonic sensors help robots detect how far away objects are. They work like bats, sending out sounds and listening for echoes.

The left side sends sound waves, and the right side listens for the echo.

How does it work?
  • The sensor sends out sound waves
  • The sound hits an object
  • The sound bounces back as an echo
  • The robot estimates how far away the object is
Think about it: What happens when the object is very close to the sensor?

Note: Ultrasonic sensors may not work well with soft objects, such as curtains, because sound does not bounce back clearly.

Why Sensors Matter

Conclusion

Sensors form the critical connection between a robot and its environment. Without sensors, a robot would be unable to gather the essential data needed to interact safely with the physical world or perceive its surroundings.

Benefits of Sensing:
  • Prevents collisions by detecting obstacles and measuring distance.
  • Provides the data necessary to plan paths through complex spaces.
  • Allows the robot to make smart decisions without human intervention.
